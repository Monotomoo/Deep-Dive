# PART I — THE JOB

## 1 · What a producer actually is

The director asks *"what is the film?"* The producer asks *"how does the film get to exist, get finished, get seen, and not destroy us on the way?"* Concretely, the producer owns:

1. **The money** — raising it, planning its arrival (cash flow), spending it against a budget, reporting it.
2. **The paper** — every right the film needs, acquired in writing, filed, provable (chain of title).
3. **The people** — hiring the team, in the right order, on signed deal memos.
4. **The schedule** — shoots, edit, finishing, festival clock, delivery dates; and the discipline when they collide.
5. **The risk** — insurance, legal review, safety, contingency; seeing the trap two moves before it springs.
6. **The relationships** — funders, broadcasters, co-producers, sales agent, festivals. The producer is who they call.
7. **The delivery** — the brutal technical/legal package that converts a finished film into paid money.

The director's currency is trust with the subjects and the truth of the cut. The producer's currency is **credibility with money** — budgets that add up, promises that get kept, paper that exists.

## 2 · Your dual-hat problem — named honestly

You are currently director + producer + DP + editor-adjacent + app developer + **sole financier**. This is how great first films get started and how they die at rough cut. What predictably fails when one person holds all hats:

- **Financing stalls during shoots** (you can't chase HAVC paperwork from a boat off Lastovo) — and shoots stall during financing.
- **The paper doesn't get done** — releases, deal memos, licenses feel skippable at midnight after a dive day. They are the sale.
- **Nobody negotiates for you** — when you're the director asking the four for life-story rights, you're also the friend; a producer can be the "bad cop" the friendship needs.
- **No one checks your budget's honesty** — solo producers under-budget post by half, always, because the shoot is vivid and the M&E track is not.
- **Burnout** — the only non-renewable resource on this production is you.

**The delegation ladder — what you hand off, in order, as money arrives:**
1. **Production management / logistics** (first euro of external money): a production manager or line-producer-days for Lastovo/USA — travel, permits, insurance, call sheets, payments. Cheapest hire, biggest hour-return.
2. **Finance & admin → the Croatian co-producer** (before the 10 Sept HAVC rok): HAVC/Eurimages administration, cost reports, audits, contracts. This is what the co-producer attach IS (→ `COPRODUCER-SHORTLIST.md`; Restart first call).
3. **The edit → a real editor** (by assembly, Nov–Dec 2026): the doc editor is the film's second author. You cannot be your own editor on an ensemble feature and keep judgment — you've seen every frame get made.
4. **Sales & festival strategy → sales agent** (from rough cut): their relationships are the product.
**What you never delegate:** the relationship with the four, the 2023 chapter's handling, final creative word, and signature authority on the company.

## 3 · Your position on the board (assets & constraints)

# PART III — THE MONEY (full detail in RESEARCH-MONEY.md)

## 6 · Budget anatomy — what €1.2M actually contains

Memorize the shape, not the lines: **production ≈ 30% · everything after the shoot ≈ 30% · "invisible" money ≈ 25%** (your fees 10%, company fee 7% — HAVC caps it there, verified NN 144/2014 — insurance/legal/E&O 3%, publicity 4%) · **contingency ≈ 9–10%** (0% contingency reads amateur, not efficient). Editing alone: ~1 month per 10 finished minutes (≈9 months for 90'), doubled tendencies for vérité. Delivery package €25–60k. E&O €10–20k for our risk profile. Archival at commercial rates ≈ $50–80/second — get 2023-footage quotes NOW, in development, not in the online.

**The two-budget strategy (be honest about scale):** real HAVC doc grants run €40–90k per film; Croatian creative docs commonly finish at €300–600k. Your €1.2–1.5M is IDFA-Forum-upper-cluster scale and only closes with the full international stack. So build both: **Budget A "the core film" ~€700–800k** — closable with HAVC + HRT + one minority partner + Eurimages + rebate + sponsors; the film exists with certainty. **Budget B "the ambition" €1.2–1.5M** — adds the second partner, bigger post, archive buy-ups, score recording, delivery war chest; unlocked by streamer/EP/market money. Every financier respects a producer who shows the film exists at A and gets bigger at B. Nobody funds a plan where the film dies without the last, least likely euro.

## 7 · The financing stack (three lanes, sequenced)

**Lane 1 — soft money (the foundation, 50–70%):** HAVC production (10 Sept; with co-producer on the prijavnica because čl. 7 scores their last 3 films) → HRT pre-sale (LOI now, call ~Dec–Feb) → IT/HU minority partners bringing their national funds → **Eurimages** (docs: ≤25% of budget, cap €500k; needs ≥2 member-state producers, ≥50% financing confirmed per country at filing, deferrals+in-kind count ≤15%; >€150k is a repayable advance from first-euro receipts at its financing share) → **Croatian rebate 25%+5%** (docs min spend €60k; apply ≥8 days before a production block WITH proof of 70% financing; pays after audit + ~90 days → it finances post/delivery, never the shoot) → city/county culture calls (€3–10k tier) → Whickers/Doc Society/Field of Vision (`US-FUNDS.md`). **Ceiling check:** GBER caps total public aid ~50% of budget, 60% for cross-border co-pros — HAVC legal confirms the math.
**Lane 2 — brands (the shoot-saver):** Mares (warm via Sanda) · Molchanovs (Lastovo teaches their curriculum) · Garmin (owns no freediving story) · Croatian pride tier · US road-trip barter (RV, GoPro). Keep <10–15% of budget, contracted as content/credits, never touching the cut, disclosed consistently.
**Lane 3 — the market (the endgame):** sales agent MG/pre-sales from rough cut · streamer acquisition post-premiere ($300k–1.5M realistic band) · Croatian theatrical + adventure tours + windowed digital as the no-streamer floor.
**Not in the stack:** equity and gap debt. Every euro of them sits above you in the waterfall forever; at doc revenue levels they usually ARE the entire net. Your no-cheap-equity instinct is arithmetic, not stubbornness.

## 8 · Cash flow — the trough

Everything pays late: HAVC in milestone tranches, rebate after audit, Eurimages in instalments (then recoups), broadcasters on delivery, the streamer years from now. A "fully financed" €1.2M film still spends quarters at **−€100–200k** cumulative. Your defenses: (1) the **cumulative cash-flow worksheet** — build it with the financing plan, update monthly, show financiers (it reads as competence); (2) sequence shoots behind confirmed tranches; (3) bridge instruments — Croatian bank discounting against a signed HAVC contract or the rebate's provisional certificate; (4) deferring your own fees (the classic float — contractually, with a defined repayment trigger); (5) your €10–25k = the personal float for exactly Lastovo+USA, already gated. **Never float payroll taxes.**

## 9 · Waterfall & CAMA — where revenue actually goes

Order: CAM fee → agent commission (doc norm 20–25%; fight for 15–20%; >30% red flag) → agent's **capped** expenses → debt → Eurimages recoupment corridor → deferrals → net split among co-producers. Worked example: €500k of sales → ≈€170k to the majority producer — normal, plan on it. **CAMA** (Freeway or Fintage; Eurimages has a Freeway framework): all buyers pay one neutral account, disbursed per the contract — the agent never touches your money. Written into the co-production agreement from day one; a sales agent who refuses a CAMA is telling you who they are.

# PART IV — THE MACHINERY (full detail in RESEARCH-LEGAL-DELIVERY.md)

## 10 · Company & chain of title

The film must be owned by the d.o.o., not by you-the-person. Every right flows to it in writing: **your own director agreement with your own company** (yes, really — copyright vests in authors; buyers look for this hole first), crew IP assignments (re-paper Krk/Sicily onto the company once registered), releases, locations, music, archive, financing agreements. **The binder discipline:** every agreement filed the day it's signed, one-line index, producible in 24 hours. Chain of title is checked at four gates — E&O, treaty certification, and the sale twice. What breaks it: the unsigned release, the freelancer who never signed, festival-only music licenses, verbal co-pro promises, contracts signed personally.

## 11 · Releases & the 2023 chapter (the legal heart of this film)

- **Everyone recognizable** signs an appearance release **on the day of filming**. Camp students at Lastovo sign the one-pager between dives (`RELEASES-DRAFT.md` Annex C).
- **The four** sign life-story-style agreements: cooperation + personal archive licensing + exclusivity + **covenant not to sue** — the clause E&O underwriters love most. Control: **never cut approval**; the defensible middle we designed honors the working-paper promise as a *viewing + factual-correction right limited to the defined 2023 Chapter* — streamer-compatible and morally straight.
- **The blackout:** specific consent to that footage (Petar + safety divers + medics). Privacy ≠ defamation — true medical facts can still violate privacy. His post-blackout interview is the context that makes use of it honest.
- **The 2023 chapter protocol:** (1) the **annotation file** — every factual assertion in the cut footnoted to a document, on-record interview, or official finding; language discipline: adjudicated fact ("sanctioned under a code of ethics; tests negative") vs allegation; (2) **right of reply** — before lock, written summaries of the specific claims to the other side (organiser, CMAS), their response or "declined" in the film, correspondence archived; (3) **legal read of the fine cut before lock** — juxtaposition can defame even when every line is true; (4) E&O bound off the back of 1–3. Done early this LOWERS the premium; done late it forces post-lock recuts, the most expensive edits in film.

## 12 · Insurance & the contracts shelf

**Two policies:** production package per shoot (gear, liability, media; dive/marine riders for us) and **E&O** ($1M/$3M limits, 3-year term, €10–20k our profile, start the application at rough cut). **The shelf, each with its non-negotiable:** director agreement (assignment + defined cuts, financeable replacement clause) · crew deal memos (economic-rights assignment + consent to modifications — drafted for Croatian law, not US "work-for-hire" boilerplate; no-injunction clause) · composer package deal (company owns composition + master, or watertight all-media perpetual license; composer keeps PRO writer's share — free to you; **stems contractually delivered**) · editor agreement (defined cuts + overages + materials custody + post-lock services) · co-production agreement (shares, spend geography, waterfall, delegate producer, credits, CAMA, final cut protected) · sales agency agreement (commission, **hard expense cap €20–40k**, 7–10yr term with performance reversion, prize money excluded, no cross-collateralization, CAMA) · license agreements (holdbacks must fit the festival/HRT plan; 10% signature / 90% **on delivery**).

## 13 · Delivery — the thing that kills films (so it won't kill ours)

The buyer's delivery schedule = picture masters (texted AND **textless** — our lower-thirds/depth-counters/subtitles make the textless build real work: do it during the online) · 5.1 + stereo + **filled M&E** (vérité's trap: dialogue and ambience share the mic; the M&E must be *built during the mix* or it costs thousands later) · stems · timecoded bilingual dialogue/spotting list · CC/SDH · complete chain of title · E&O cert · cue sheet · credits/billing with **HAVC/Eurimages logo obligations** · 50–200 stills (**shot during production — Etna cannot be re-staged**) · layered key art · QC pass (fails on loudness, sync, archival artifacts, M&E holes; failed QC = 4–8 lost weeks). Realistic cost **€25–60k, 2–4 months.** Our prevention, standing orders: streamer spec in front of the post supervisor before any post contract; M&E/textless/stems/spotting in the sound-house and online deal memos; 5% of post held as delivery contingency; stills assigned on every remaining shoot starting Lastovo.

# PART V — THE MARKET

## 14 · Post-production as strategy (not just craft)

Paper edit from the interview architecture (the five returning questions ARE the string-out structure — the app's interview library is built for this) → assembly (editor hired Nov–Dec; cut AWAY from your attachment: you shot it, the editor owes the footage nothing) → rough cut (spring 2027) → **feedback screenings**: small, structured, strangers included, written questions before discussion; run 2–3 of them — films that skip this premiere their blind spots → fine cut → edit lab window (IDFAcademy / Ex Oriente / Sundance Edit & Story Lab — labs are also where ARTE and agents fall in love) → **legal read** → lock → finishing. The bigger swings (physiological score, real-time dive, four-voices monologue) get tested at rough cut and killed without mercy if they don't play — the bible's own rule.

## 15 · Festival → sale mechanics

**Premiere status is currency**: world > international > European; a top-tier world premiere is bought once and never refunded — nothing public before the premiere festival clears it. **The sequence:** IDFA Forum (Nov 2026, film unfinished — financing room, not premiere) → agent courted from sizzle, formally attached at rough cut (they now board early and may bring money/editorial notes — normal, cap their expenses) → Cannes Docs market screenings (May 2027; market ≠ premiere, status safe) → **world premiere Telluride/TIFF Sept 2027** (Plan B: Tribeca June; Plan C: CPH:DOX/IDFA 2027 as premiere instead of European slot) → buyers watch at the festival and on screeners; deals close **weeks to months after** (The Perfect Neighbor pattern) — no in-festival bidding panic, the agent works the room → then the genre circuit with no status cost: Banff (→ world tour), Kendal, ocean tours, FICTS — the *Alpinist* long-tail engine → Croatian theatrical event release (four national heroes = real domestic box office) → windowed digital if no global buy.
**Buyers, ranked for us:** Netflix (bought freediving twice; Deepest Breath ~8M views/2 wks; acquisitions via Del Deo, sports via Spitzer — access+stakes+characters is their stated triad) · Nat Geo (Free Solo/Rescue heritage) · HBO/Max · ARTE La Lucarne / ZDF / YLE via the co-producer · Red Bull Studios as co-financier (price = control). **Pitch as adventure-thriller-with-heart in the Free Solo lane — never "issue doc," never "another Deepest Breath"** (ours: alive, present-tense, ensemble, vindication).

## 16 · The EP move

*The Deepest Breath* sold to Netflix before its premiere because of the package (Battsek/A24). Our equivalent: after the premiere strategy is set and the sizzle is strong, ask the sales agent to matchmake **one respected adventure-doc EP** (the Chin/Vasarhelyi, Noah Media, Ventureland tier). Cost: a fee and a credit. Return: the streamer's phone answers. Timing: rough cut, from strength — never now, at maximum weakness.

# PART VI — EXECUTION

## 17 · The traps, each with its guard (first-timer killers → Deep Dive answers)

1. **Overshooting without structure** → the five returning questions are the structure; log/select after every block in the app; paper edit before the editor arrives.
2. **Missing paper** → releases at Lastovo; binder discipline; re-paper Krk/Sicily; lawyer this month.
3. **Post & delivery unbudgeted** → the 30%-after-the-shoot rule; €25–60k delivery line; 5% delivery contingency — in Budget A, not just B.
4. **No E&O until the buyer asks** → application at rough cut; annotation file grows from Lastovo onward, not retro-fitted.
5. **Blown premiere status** → nothing from the film public pre-premiere; sizzle stays in private links; BTS social ≠ film footage.
6. **No sales strategy until the film is done** → agent conversations start at IDFA 2026 with the sizzle.
7. **Money runs out in post** → cash-flow worksheet monthly; rebate banked for post; edit begins only with the edit financed.
8. **The uncapped sales agent / cross-collateralization / no CAMA** → §12 checklist; walk away is a position.
9. **Solo-producer burnout** → the delegation ladder (§2) is calendared like a deadline: PM days by August, co-producer by September, editor by November.
10. **The sensitive chapter handled late** → right-of-reply and legal read are IN the schedule (pre-lock), owned, budgeted.

## 18 · Operating cadence (how you run this from tomorrow)

- **The producer hour, daily at fixed time:** paper, money, email — before creative work eats the day.
- **Weekly:** cash position vs worksheet · pipeline (every open application/conversation, one line each) · binder check (what got signed this week?).
- **Per shoot block:** pre-block (insurance, permits, releases printed, stills assigned, two-copies backup rule) · post-block (footage logged, releases filed, costs reported, one-page "what the film gained" note).
- **Monthly:** cost report (budget vs actual vs estimate-to-complete) — the habit that makes HAVC/Eurimages audits trivial and financiers calm.
- **The app is the production office:** schedule, contacts, releases tracker, interview library, sponsor pipeline, archive log. What isn't in the system doesn't exist.
